## TL;DR

Use Slack for quick questions, GitHub for code discussions, Jira for task updates. Default to async communication. Respect deep work time. If it's not urgent, it can wait.

## Channel Expectations

### Slack

Slack is our primary communication tool, but it shouldn't be a constant interruption. Use it thoughtfully.

**Response expectations:**
- **Direct messages:** Respond within 2 hours during work hours
- **Channel mentions (@you):** Respond within 4 hours
- **General channel posts:** Read daily, respond if relevant

**Best practices:**
- Use threads to keep conversations organised
- Don't use @channel or @here unless it's genuinely urgent
- Set your status when you're in deep work, on a call, or out of office
- Mute channels that aren't relevant to your current work

### GitHub

All code-related discussions happen on GitHub — in PR comments, issue threads, or discussions.

- **PR reviews:** Respond within 4 hours (see Code Review Guidelines)
- **Issue comments:** Respond within 1 business day
- Don't discuss code architecture in Slack — move it to a GitHub issue where it's searchable and permanent

### Jira

Keep your tickets updated. At minimum:

- Move tickets to the correct status when you start or finish work
- Add a brief comment if a ticket is blocked or delayed
- Log time if you're spending significantly more or less than estimated

## Deep Work

We believe in protecting focus time. Every team member should have at least **4 hours of uninterrupted work time** per day.

**Guidelines:**
- Don't expect instant responses to non-urgent messages
- Batch your questions — send one message with multiple items instead of a stream of notifications
- If someone's Slack status shows they're in deep work, don't DM unless it's blocking you

## Meetings

### Rules

- **Every meeting needs an agenda** — no agenda, no meeting
- **Meetings are 25 or 50 minutes** — never 30 or 60. The buffer prevents back-to-back burnout
- **Camera on is encouraged, not required** — some days you just don't feel like it
- **If it can be a Slack message, don't book a meeting** — meetings are expensive (10 people × 30 min = 5 hours of company time)

### Standing Meetings

| Meeting | Frequency | Duration | Who |
|---------|-----------|----------|-----|
| Sprint Planning | Bi-weekly Monday | 50 min | Whole team |
| Daily Standup | Async in Slack | — | Whole team |
| Retro | Bi-weekly Friday | 50 min | Whole team |
| 1:1s | Weekly | 25 min | Manager + report |
| Design Review | Weekly | 50 min | Engineering + Design |

## Urgency Levels

When you need something, be explicit about urgency:

- **Urgent (production issue):** Post in #incidents, DM the on-call engineer, and call if no response in 10 minutes
- **High (blocking your work):** DM the person directly on Slack and follow up after 2 hours
- **Normal (needs input):** Post in the relevant channel or comment on the ticket
- **Low (FYI):** Post in Slack or add to the next meeting agenda

## Why This Matters

We're a small team. Effective communication is the difference between moving fast and stepping on each other's toes. These norms aren't bureaucracy — they're how we respect each other's time while keeping information flowing.
